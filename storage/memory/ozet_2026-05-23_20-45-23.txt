Salam! - This is a more informal greeting, similar to "hello" or "hi," often used among friends or peers. It's less formal but still polite in Turkish-speaking contexts.

2. Sabahın uydayım/gece ayın çok güzel mi? – This translates to "Good morning" or "How is your day?" and reflects well wishes for the current time of day, showing care in Turkish culture.

3. Başarılar! - A simple way to wish someone good luck without specifying any contextual circumstances; it's equivalent to saying "good luck."

4. Uzerinden geldiklerimle güyle gitmişim (I came from afar) – This is a polite form of greeting, acknowledging the effort and journey when arriving at someone’s location in Turkish culture.

5. Başın bazen ders aldığı için İyi günler! - A formal farewell equivalent to "Have you had your lesson?" It's used upon departure as a polite and respectful acknowledgment of someone’nereducation effort.

6. Özür dilerim (I am sorry) – Used when seeking forgiveness or needing to apologize; it conveys politeness and humility in Turkish culture, similar to "excuse me."
